Museo Nacional de Antropología
World-class anthropology museum
Culture
🚗 15 min taxi/Uber ⏰ 9am–6pm 💰 $210 MXN ea ⏱️ Plan 3–4 hrs
Chapultepec Park · Maps ↗
The crown jewel. Don't rush — the Aztec & Maya halls alone are worth the trip.
Details ▾
Finding the entranceMain entrance is on Av. Mahatma Gandhi in Chapultepec Park. Look for the massive stone Tlaloc monolith outside. If arriving by Uber, ask to be dropped at the Calzada Mahatma Gandhi side — calmer and closer to ticket counters.
TipsArrive by 9am to beat lines. Large bags must be checked at free lockers near the entrance. Restrooms are only near the entrance — go before starting your tour. The Aztec and Maya halls are the most popular; start there if short on time. Closed Mondays.

MODO — Museo del Objeto
Quirky design & everyday objects museum
Culture
🚶 ~5 min from apartment ⏰ 10am–6pm 💰 $60 MXN
Colima 145, Roma Norte · Maps ↗
Quick 45-min visit, right in the neighborhood. Mooni is half a block away — hit both.
Details ▾
Finding the entranceOccupies one of only eight remaining Art Nouveau buildings in Mexico City, built in 1906. The ornate facade on Colima street is unmistakable — look for the decorative stonework entrance.
TipsExhibitions rotate regularly, connecting everyday objects to culture. The collection spans 100,000+ objects from 19th century to present. Small museum — 45 min to 1 hr is plenty. Combine with Mooni at Colima 129 right around the corner.

9:00 AM — Morning
Chapultepec Castle
Hilltop castle with panoramic city views
Culture
🚗 15 min to park 🚶 + 20 min uphill walk ⏰ 9am–5pm 💰 $210 MXN ea
Chapultepec Hill · Maps ↗
Arrive at 9am opening. Plan ~90 minutes including the hike up.
Details ▾
Finding the entranceAccess is on foot up a steep paved hill from Bosque de Chapultepec. Look for the Monumento a los Niños Héroes at the base — the walking path starts there. A small tram runs from near the base if you want to skip the uphill walk. For Uber, ask to be dropped near the Estela de Luz monument.
TipsNo large bags, food, or drinks inside — lockers available near entrance. Bring water for the uphill walk but finish it before entering. Photography allowed without flash. Best to arrive right at 9am opening on a weekday.

Durango 200, Roma Norte · Maps ↗
Walk-in — arrive right at noon when doors open. The tuna tostadas and grilled whole fish are legendary.
Details ▾
Finding the entranceLarge, open doorway along Durango street. You'll spot it by the line of people waiting outside during peak lunch hours. Nearest Metro: Sevilla (Line 1).
TipsLunch only (roughly 1pm–6pm, closed evenings). Arrive right at noon to avoid the serious wait. The tuna tostada and pescado a la talla (red/green grilled whole fish) are the must-orders. Cash and card accepted. Opened in 1998 by chef Gabriela Cámara.

Flea Market → Biblioteca Vasconcelos → Centro Histórico
9:00 AM — Market
Tianguis La Lagunilla
Massive Sunday flea market — antiques, art, vintage
Market
🚗 15–20 min Uber ⏰ ~9am–5pm ⏱️ Plan 2–3 hrs 💰 Free entry
Near Eje 1 Norte, Lagunilla · Maps ↗
Arrive early (9–10am) for best selection and fewer crowds. Bring cash. Bargain!
Details ▾
Finding itThe Sunday tianguis sprawls along streets near Av. Paseo de la Reforma in Colonia Morelos. Not one building — look for the massive overflow of street vendors taking over entire blocks. Nearest Metro: Garibaldi-Lagunilla (Line B).
TipsBring cash in small bills — most vendors don't take cards. Bargaining is expected. Watch your belongings — keep phone in front pocket. You'll find antique furniture, vintage records, first-edition books, clothing, electronics, and folk art. Wear comfortable shoes — uneven surfaces everywhere.
11:30 AM — Library
Biblioteca Vasconcelos
Stunning "megabiblioteca" — must visit
Culture
🚶 ~20 min walk from market ⏰ 8:30am–7:30pm daily 💰 Free ⏱️ 45 min–1 hr
Eje 1 Norte, Buenavista · Maps ↗
~1.5 km walk from La Lagunilla. Both are in the north part of the city — pair perfectly together. Jaw-dropping architecture.
Details ▾
Finding the entranceMain entrance faces a garden on Eje 1 Norte in the Buenavista neighborhood. Nearest Metro: Buenavista station. Security guards check bags at entrance; backpacks must be checked on the first floor before going upstairs.
TipsCompletely free. Keep quiet — it's a working library. Photography allowed for personal use, no flash. No food or drink inside. Bring a light jacket — cool airflow in some areas. Look for Gabriel Orozco's whale skeleton ("Ballena") hanging in the center.
1:00 PM — Walk South Through Centro
Plaza de la Constitución (Zócalo)
Main square — heart of Mexico City
Culture
🚗 10 min Uber from library
Maps ↗
Uber or walk south from Vasconcelos through Centro to the massive Zócalo.
Details ▾
Finding itOpen public square — no entrance or ticket needed. Bordered by the Metropolitan Cathedral (north), National Palace (east), and government buildings (south). Nearest Metro: Zócalo (Line 2), exits directly onto the plaza.
TipsThe massive Mexican flag at the center is lowered at 6pm daily in a ceremonial display. The National Palace (free, Tue–Sun) with Diego Rivera murals is right on the east side. Street food vendors around the edges. This is the heart of Centro Histórico.
1:30 PM — Museum
Templo Mayor Museum
Aztec ruins right next to the Zócalo
Culture
🚶 Adjacent to Zócalo ⏰ 9am–5pm 💰 Free on Sundays! ⏱️ 1–1.5 hrs
Seminario St, next to Cathedral · Maps ↗
Free admission on Sundays. Incredible Aztec archaeology. Right next to Zócalo.
Details ▾
Finding the entranceEntrance is on Seminario Street, just northeast of the Zócalo and to the right of the Metropolitan Cathedral. The entrance leads downward into the archaeological site. Nearest Metro: Zócalo (Line 2).
TipsPlan 1.5–2 hrs for both the outdoor ruins and the indoor museum. Exhibit labels are mostly in Spanish — bring a translation app. Non-flash photography allowed. The outdoor ruins have no shade; bring sunscreen and a hat. Free on Sundays for everyone.
